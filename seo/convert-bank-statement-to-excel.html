How to Convert a Bank Statement PDF to Excel

Most people receive their bank statements as PDF files — either emailed to them or downloaded from online banking. Converting these PDFs to Excel manually would take hours. StatementExcel uses AI to do it in seconds.

Quickstart: Go to the converter tool, upload your PDF, enter your Claude API key, and click Convert. You'll have a clean Excel-ready CSV in under 30 seconds.

Step-by-Step: Convert Bank Statement to Excel

Step 1: Download Your Bank Statement as PDF

Log into your bank's online portal and download your statement as a PDF. Choose "e-statement" or "PDF" format — not a printed/scanned copy. Text-based PDFs from bank portals work perfectly. Image-based (scanned paper) PDFs do not work with any PDF converter.

Step 2: Get a Free Claude API Key

StatementExcel uses the Anthropic Claude API to read your PDF. You'll need a free API key from console.anthropic.com. New accounts include free credits — more than enough to convert dozens of statements. Your key is saved in your browser and never sent to our servers.

Step 3: Upload and Convert

Drop your PDF onto the converter, paste your API key, and click Convert. The AI extracts every transaction — date, description, amount, reference number, running balance — and presents them in a live preview table.

Step 4: Choose Your Excel Layout

Pick from four layouts before downloading:

  • Standard — All columns, single amount with +/− sign
  • Split Columns — Withdrawals and deposits in separate columns (Chase bank style)
  • Accountant — Absolute values with DR/CR labels, ideal for bookkeeping
  • Minimal — Date, description, amount only — clean for importing into MYOB, Xero, or QuickBooks

Step 5: Download as CSV or Excel

Click "Download CSV" — the file opens directly in Excel, Google Sheets, or Numbers. The BOM character (byte order mark) is included so Excel reads special characters correctly.

Note: This works with text-based PDFs from online banking portals. It does not work with scanned paper statements or image PDFs. If you're unsure, try it — you'll see an error message if the PDF can't be parsed.

Why Convert Bank Statements to Excel?

Once your bank statement is in Excel, you can:

  • Filter and sort transactions by date, category, or merchant
  • Create pivot tables to summarize monthly spending
  • Import into accounting software like Xero, MYOB, or QuickBooks
  • Reconcile accounts and track budgets
  • Prepare documentation for tax returns, loans, or visa applications
  • Build dashboards and spending analysis charts
